Reuben

Description

The Reuben sandwich is a North American grilled sandwich composed of corned beef, Swiss cheese, sauerkraut, and Russian dressing or Thousand Island dressing, grilled between slices of rye bread. It is associated with kosher-style delicatessens but is not kosher, as it combines meat and cheese

Ingredients

  • Rye Bread & Butter: Rye bread spread with butter results in toasty slices that are just non-negotiable when it comes to a perfect Reuben sandwich
  • Russian Dressing: Skip the store-bought bottle if possible—our homemade Russian dressing is truly something special. Here, we’re combining horseradish, mayo, ketchup, Worcestershire sauce, salt, pepper, paprika, and a bit of sugar for an easy (and delicious) dressing. That being said, we get creative in other recipes by adding garlic powder and relish. This is all about personal preference, so start with our recipe here, then experiment based on personal taste.
  • Swiss Cheese: Swiss cheese adds a mild but noticeable flavor to the sandwich, and when melted, adds an almost creamy element.
  • Corned Beef: If you have any leftover corned beef, this sandwich is the perfect way to use it up, but you can always make our best-ever corned beef brisket. No time to make corned beef? No prob—you can easily pick some up at your local deli or grocery store.
  • Sauerkraut: A Reuben isn’t a Reuben without crunchy, salty, and sour sauerkraut. Feel free to use your favorite brand here, and if you need ideas for leftovers, check out our top sauerkraut recipes

Steps

  1. Once you have your corned beef and Russian dressing ready to go, this sandwich is a breeze to create. First, butter one side of each slice of bread, then spread Russian dressing on the non-butter side of each slice. Layer on the cheese, corned beef, and sauerkraut on half of the slices, then top each sandwich with the remaining slice, dressing side down.
  2. Heat a skillet over medium heat, then carefully place the prepared sandwich in the skillet. Cook until the bread is toasty and golden and the cheese has melted. This will take around 3 minutes per side, but keep an eye on it.
